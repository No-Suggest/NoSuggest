Why it matters

What Makes NoSuggest Different

Other tools organise YouTube. NoSuggest removes the parts of YouTube that are designed to keep you watching longer than you intended.

Zero recommendations

No "Up next", no sidebar suggestions, no homepage feed of random videos. You see only the channels you subscribed to — nothing more.

No account needed

Your subscriptions are saved on your device in local storage. No Google login, no sign-up form, no data sent to any server about who you are.

Kids mode with PIN

Lock NoSuggest to a curated list of children's channels with a PIN. No YouTube Shorts, no random videos, no way for kids to break out without the code.

Installs like an app

Add NoSuggest to your home screen on iPhone or Android. It opens full screen, feels like a native app, and works on any device — no App Store needed.

Official YouTube player

Videos play through YouTube's official embed — full quality, subtitles, and your YouTube Premium benefits if you have them. No shady third-party streams.

Always free

No premium tier, no ads, no subscription. NoSuggest is a quiet indie project built for people who just want a calmer way to watch YouTube.

Head to head

NoSuggest vs Each Alternative

Every alternative solves a different problem. Here's the honest picture.

NoSuggest vs YouTube Subscriptions

YouTube's own subscription feed is better than the homepage, but it still surfaces recommendations, still autoplays, still shows Shorts between your subscriptions, and still tracks everything you watch to feed the algorithm. NoSuggest shows only the latest videos from channels you chose — in chronological order, with no suggestions added. It's the subscription feed YouTube should have built.

NoSuggest vs PocketTube

PocketTube is a popular browser extension that organises your YouTube subscriptions into groups. It's genuinely useful — but it still runs inside YouTube, still requires your Google account, and still shows YouTube's recommendations and Shorts. It's an organiser, not an escape from the algorithm. NoSuggest is completely separate from YouTube's interface, requires no account, and collects no data.

NoSuggest vs FreeTube

FreeTube is an excellent open-source desktop app for Windows, Mac, and Linux. If you only watch YouTube on a desktop and want maximum privacy, FreeTube is worth considering. But it requires downloading and installing an app, doesn't work on mobile, and uses a third-party player. NoSuggest works instantly on any device — mobile or desktop — with no installation, and plays videos through YouTube's official player so quality and subtitles are always perfect.

NoSuggest vs Invidious

Invidious is an open-source YouTube front-end focused on privacy — no tracking, no JavaScript from Google. The catch: it requires finding a working public instance (many are slow or down), or self-hosting your own server. Video quality and reliability vary. NoSuggest works instantly at nosuggest.com with no setup, and uses YouTube's own player so reliability is never an issue.
